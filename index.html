Projects

Web Development

Love Running

A user friendly and responsive website advertising the conceptual company: Love Running. Love Running is targetted at people who love running in groups. The website has a contact modal form and gallery.

Built using HTML and CSS.

Whiskey Drop

A user friendly and responsive website advertising the conceptual company: Whiskey Drop. The idea of the site is to provide a service in which whiskey is delivered to the consumer's door. Among many other features, the site has a responsive navbar and a sign-up modal.

Built using HTML, CSS and Bootstrap.

Skyrim Modding Guide

This site is an on-going personal project aimed at pc-gamers who wish to modify their game. The game in question is Skyrim - the most popular game within the modding community. The site is intended to function as a guide, providing users with an easy-to-navigate user interface to help them achieve the best setup for their game.

Built using HTML, CSS and a little Javascript.

This site is currently under maintenance and is unavailable for viewing.

Projects

Academic Work

Dissertation

Lear in Mourning:
Grief and Identity in Shakespeare's, Eyre's and Edel's King Lear

My dissertation, of which I achieved a first on, explores the relationship between Lear's madness and his identity as an old man, father and king. It considers the relationship between these identities and the impact it has on his grief.

Translation Project

In this project I translate extracts from '宛如昨日：生存游戏', Just Like Yesterday: Survival Game, a crime fiction novel by Cai Jun. It also includes a translation commentary - the theory and method behind the translating process.

Hamlet
Editing Task

In this project, I was apart of a team of three, assigned with the task of editing a scene from Hamlet and then discussing the the rationale and creative process behind the editing process.

Henry V
Shakespeare: Resistant to Adaptation?

This essay discusses whether or not Shakespeare's plays show a resistance to adaptation. My focus was on Henry V, and in this essay I delve into the limitations of stage and the new medium of the film industry, and what that tells us about Shakespeare as an artist.
